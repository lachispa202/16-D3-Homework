Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

Health insurance coverage seems higher in states with a lower percentage of poverty.

There appears to be a slight trend between higher rates of poverty and higher percentage of people obese and or who smoke. However, it does not appear to be a very strong correlation as the distribution of data points within the chart fit one distinctive line.

Regarding age, there appears to be no clear trend between insurance, smoking, and obesity. Geography might impact some of correlations meaning states with higher frequency of smoking may have a greater amount of obesity. However, there will always be exceptions which includes Texas that has a lower frequency of smoking yet has a higher obeserved rate of obesity.

Finally, there is clear trend is observed for household income in which higher the income results in lower frequency of lacking healthcare, lower frequancy of smoking, and less obesity.
